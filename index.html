Dublin Dogos RFC is a new club formed by expats and Irish players with the aim to foster integration through rugby under the slogan “one club, all countries”.

The team functions as a “permanent Barbarian” team, combining talent from different nationalities to compete at the highest level and bring together the expats and rugby communities in Ireland.

The club is a members-owned organisation and is run by committee which is drawn from a range of sectors including accountancy, financial services, retail, information technology and public service professionals.

"My name is Diego Menendez, Chairman and one of the founders of Dublin Dogos RFC. I am 43 years old, retail and PR manager by trade. I've started to play rugby in Argentina, then played in Wales and also in England Rugby Union and Rugby League"

"I was born in Antrim, played there through school and into senior ball. I moved away and found it hard to find a rugby home around Dublin area. So with the diverse amount of players that Dogos RFC have, there was only one team I wanted to be involved with."
